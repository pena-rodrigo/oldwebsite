In summary,

  • Since 2019, I am a postdoc working at the lab of Prof. Horacio Rotstein at the New Jersey Institute of Technology and Rutgers University.

  • I obtained my PhD from the University of São Paulo. There, I worked with Prof. Antonio Carlos Roque in Computational Neuroscience since I was an undergraduate. I was part of the lab SisNe. During this time I was able to work in many projects on Computational Neuroscience.

  • I was also part of the NeuroMat group from the University of São Paulo. There, I was able to interact with Mathematicians and we developed some brain models involving stochastic processes.

  • In 2015, as a PhD candidate, I was awarded funding from the São Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP) to spend a year at the Humboldt University in Berlin. At that time I worked with Prof. Benjamin Lindner and Prof. Michael Zaks. Check Publications to see some of the papers we published.

  • During my PhD I was part of the International Research Training Group (IRTG) 1740. Because of that, I traveled many times to Berlin and learned a lot by interacting with researchers at the Humboldt University and at the Potsdam University. In particular, I learned a lot of Dynamical System's and Information theoryat this time.

  • In 2012, as an undergraduate, I was awarded a scholarship to study one year abroad and I chose to go to the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in Switzerland where I could learn much more about Neuroscience and Computational Neuroscience. Furthermore, at the time I worked with graph theory under the supervision of Prof. Christian Gaumier at EPFL.

  • Throughout my career, I have also interacted with many labs that focus works on electrophysiological recordings. I have already proposed models to explain those data as well as how to extract information from these data. Specifically, the paper "Modeling hippocampal CA1 GABAergic synapses of audiogenic rats" published at the International Journal of Neural Systems was in the media in many important national newspapers including UOL VivaBem which is a Brazilian web newspaper content and the fifth most visited website in Brazil (data from 2012).
